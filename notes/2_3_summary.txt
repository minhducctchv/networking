The data goes through the air. [pause]

We call this wireless. [pause]

There are three common media. [pause]

Let's look at them. [pause]

Number one is copper. [pause]

Copper is a metal cable. [pause]

Copper is cheap. [pause]

Copper is easy to use. [pause]

But copper has a problem. [pause]

It is sensitive to noise. [pause]

We call this noise "EMI." [pause]

Copper is also short. [pause]

It works for about 100 meters. [pause]

Not more. [pause]

Number two is fiber optic. [pause]

Fiber uses light to send data. [pause]

Light is not affected by EMI. [pause]

This is good. [pause]

Fiber is fast. [pause]

It has high bandwidth. [pause]

Fiber can go very far. [pause]

Many kilometers. [pause]

But fiber is expensive. [pause]

Number three is wireless. [pause]

Wireless sends data through the air. [pause]

Wireless is very flexible. [pause]

You can move around. [pause]

But wireless is not stable. [pause]

And wireless needs encryption. [pause]

Encryption keeps your data safe. [pause]

So, which one is the best? [pause]

There is no perfect answer. [pause]

You must choose. [pause]

You think about speed, distance, cost, and noise. [pause]

The first word is bandwidth. [pause]

Bandwidth is the maximum speed. [pause]

It is the top limit. [pause]

It is a number in theory. [pause]

The second word is throughput. [pause]

Throughput is the real speed. [pause]

The speed you really get. [pause]

Throughput is often lower than bandwidth. [pause]

Why? [pause]

Because of the slowest part of the path. [pause]

We call the slowest part the "bottleneck." [pause]

The third word is latency. [pause]

Latency is delay. [pause]

Latency has four parts. [pause]

One, transmission delay. [pause]

Two, propagation delay. [pause]

Three, queuing delay. [pause]

Four, processing delay. [pause]

Together, they make latency. [pause]

More bandwidth makes the transmission delay small. [pause]

How? [pause]

You can use a CDN. [pause]

This is often better than more bandwidth. [pause]

The fifth idea is the bandwidth-delay product. [pause]

It is the data "in the air" right now. [pause]

Data on the way. [pause]

The signal gets weak over distance. [pause]
